How to be Sucessfully Organized

Time Needed: 30 Minutes or Hours
Effectivness: Very

Whether you're a Journalist, Banker, McDonalds Managar or even a student, work and assignments can begin to feel stressful. Project after project task after task, it can be hard to keep up with all that you have to do. Falling behind is something that happens to many people because of the lack of organization. Being organized is crucial to winning in life and if you want to learn how to be orgnaized read to find out how!

  • Things You'll Need

  • Notebook
  • Pens/Pencils
  • Planner/Schedule
  • Sticky Notes
  • Thumbtack/Tape
  • Your Favorite Snack

  1. Find out what is most important.

    Understanding your priorities is the first and most important step towards organization. A good way to do this is to track and find what specifically your tasks and goals are. Whether they are long term or short term write them all down on a piece of paper. Alternatively you may type all of this on an online document, the point is to actually physically see your tasks Compare deadlines, difficulty and importance of each task to rank on which to focus on first. Once you've accomplished this then everything else will go smoothly.

  2. Make to do lists.

    Once you have sorted out your priorities, start making daily to do lists. Knowing and seeing what tasks you need to accomplish by the end of the day will keep you motivated. It doesn't have to be large tasks or goals but steps in order to complete them. The satisfaction of checking off tasks from your list will make you want to achieve more, creative a healthy habit and cycle of getting things done. Once you get used to daily to-do lists move on to weekly and then monthly. This will ensure that you are on track to achieve your large goals and your taksks.

    A to-do list doesn't have to be fancy. All you need is visualization

  3. Use a Schedule or Planner.

    Now that you have to-do lists, you'll next need to visualize what your days/week is going to look like. A schedule or planner goes into depth on the specific time of day you'll be working on tasks. You'll see where deadlines are and how much time you can allocate to focus on a specific task. You will also be able to track how much time you have inbetween to take a break. Ensure that you either purchase a planner/schedule or make one yourself using your paper and pencils or pens.

    More on how to make your weekly schedule.

  4. Take breaks.

    It's important to remember that while working on your tasks that you need to stop and take breaks as to not burn out. Burining out is some of the most common issues people run into trying to juggle all their work. Ensure that you are setting time aside for yourself throughout the day. These breaks can be as long as you see fit, you choose what is best for you. This is to make sure that you are working as efficiently as possible and you are pacing yourself as to not overwhelm or give you unecessary stress.

  5. Clean your workspace/enviorment.

    A clear enviorment is a clear mind. When minimizing the distractions around you this also includes the mess. This will promote a focused mind since you don't have trash lying around that could cause you to stop and do something else. Choosing your workspace can a also aid in minimizing the amount of trash you have in your area. Working at an office or a desk can benefit you more than working in your bedroom or your bed.

  6. Reward yourself.

    When you complete a task or maybe you've gone through a sucessful week of tasks make sure to reward yourself to help you stay motivated. For the small things have your favorite snack, or order your favorite food. Set some time aside to do your favorite hobbies. For much larger accomplihments go out and invite some friends. Make sure that you are not indulging on anythign and that the reward you give yourself weigh as equally as the task.
